TEHRAN – The Turkish foreign minister visited Tehran on Wednesday to hold talks with his counterpart Mohammad Javad Zarif and President Hassan Rouhani.

 “Visiting Tehran. Had a fruitful meeting with Foreign Minister @JZarif. Will also meet President @HassanRouhani,” Mevlut Cavusoglu tweeted after his meeting with Zarif. According to media, the Iranian and Turkish officials held talks on bilateral, regional and international issues, including the results of a recent conference in Sochi on the settlement of crisis in Syria. Iran has criticized Turkish military operation in northwestern Syria, saying it is a violation of the Syrian territorial integrity. SP/PA
