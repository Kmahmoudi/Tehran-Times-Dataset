TEHRAN – Iran’s non-oil exports to China stood at $6.1 billion in January-November period of 2017, registering a 27 percent increase compared to the preceding year’s same time span. According to Iran’s Trade Promotion Organization (TPO) office of public relations, oil exports considered, Iran’s trade balance with China registered $400 million surplus during the mentioned period, IRIB news reported on Wednesday. The two countries total trade is estimated to be $37 billion for 2017, of which over $7 billion is the share of non-oil commodities.
