TEHRAN – Iran exported over $3 billion worth of goods to South Korea during the first nine months of the current Iranian calendar year (March 21-December 21, 2017) up 30 percent compared to its preceding year. Meanwhile, Iran shipped in some $2.48 billion worth of goods from the Asian country, IRNA reported on Tuesday quoting an official at Trade Promotion Organization (TPO) of Iran.
As reported, foraldehyde, acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene copolymers, gypsum, slag wax, white cement, floor coverings and dates were among top exported items to South Korea. EF/MG
