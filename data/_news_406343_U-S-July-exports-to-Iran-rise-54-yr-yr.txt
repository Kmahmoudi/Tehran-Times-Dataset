TEHRAN- The exports of the U.S. to Iran in the seventh month of 2016 witnessed a 54 percent hike and stood at $25.9 million, compared to July 2015, Tasnim news agency reported. According to the latest report of American Statistical Association (ASA), Iran-U.S. commercial exchange in the first seven months of 2016 experienced an 11 percent decline and shrank to $139.4 million from its previous $157.7 million in the same time span in 2015.
The worth of U.S.’s exports to Iran in January-July 2016 was $97.7 million, showing a 38 percent fall from $157.7 million in the same period in 2015.
The country’s worth of imports from the Islamic Republic in the said time was $41.7 million, while the figure stood at zero in the same time in the preceding year. HJ/MA All Content by Mehr News Agency is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.
