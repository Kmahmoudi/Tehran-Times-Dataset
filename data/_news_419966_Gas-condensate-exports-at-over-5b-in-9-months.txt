TEHRAN- Iran exported $5.098 billion worth of gas condensate during the first nine months of the current Iranian calendar year (March 21-Decemeber 21, 2017), IRIB news reported on Tuesday.
 Gas condensate accounts for the lion’s share of Iran’s exports during the nine-month period, according to the Islamic Republic of Iran Customs Administration (IRICA). The value of exports from Iran stood at $31.64 billion during the first nine months of this year, falling 2.39 percent from the last year’s figure. Gas condensate, liquefied propane followed by methanol and low-density oils and finally hematite granules were among top exported commodities from Iran. China, United Araba Emirates, Iraq, South Korea, and Afghanistan were the top importers of Iranian commodities during the nine-month period. MA
