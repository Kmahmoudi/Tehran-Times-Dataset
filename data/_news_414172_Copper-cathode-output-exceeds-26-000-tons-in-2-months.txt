TEHRAN – Iran’s copper cathode output has exceeded 26,000 tons during the first two months of the current Iranian calendar year (March 20-May 21, 2017), IRNA reported on Sunday. Based on the report, the figure fell near 12 percent compared to the same period last year.
Iran has put the annual production of 450,000 tons of copper cathode on the agenda by the end of its outlook plan for the calendar year of 1404 (ends on March 20, 2026). EF/MA
