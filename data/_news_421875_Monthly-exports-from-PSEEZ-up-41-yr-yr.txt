TEHRAN- The value of exports from Pars Special Economic Energy Zone (PSEEZ) rose by 41 percent during the Iranian calendar month of Bahman (January 21-February 19) compared to the same month last year, Shana reported. According to Mohammad Mehdi Mahdavi, the director of customs affairs at PSEEZ, exports of gas condensate and non-oil commodities from the region totaled 2.684 tons worth $1.436 billion in the mentioned period. The exports increased by 20 percent in terms of weight, the official said. The value of exports from PSEEZ rose by 22 percent in the past Iranian calendar year (March 2016-March 2017).
