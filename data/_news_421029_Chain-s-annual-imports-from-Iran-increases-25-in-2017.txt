TEHRAN- Iran’s exports to China increased 25.2 percent in 2017 in comparison to the preceding year, Public Relations Department of Iran Trade Promotion Organization (TPO) quoted one of the organization’s officials.  “China imported $18.58 billion of goods from Iran in 2017,” Hossein Rustaiee announced.  “Crude oil, various types of polyethylene, methanol, sulfur, styrene, iron ore, copper ore, chrome ore, lead ore, zinc ore, marble and travertine were among the major products exported to China in the said year,” he added.  HJ/MA
