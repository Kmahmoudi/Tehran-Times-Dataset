TEHRAN – Iran’s deputy foreign minister for Asia and Pacific affairs held separate talks with Afghan President Ashraf Ghani and Chief Executive Officer Abdullah Abdullah in Kabul on Tuesday, IRNA reported.

 In his meeting with Ghani, Ebrahim Rahimpour offered his condolences over the recent terrorist attacks in Kabul and condemned acts of terrorism committed by extremist groups in the neighboring country. The Afghan president, for his part, stressed the need to promote the relations between Iran and Afghanistan in all spheres. SP/PA
