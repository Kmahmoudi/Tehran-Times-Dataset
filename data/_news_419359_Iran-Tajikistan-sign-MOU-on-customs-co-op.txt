TEHRAN- Iran and Tajikistan signed an MOU on customs cooperation at the place of the World Customs Organization (WCO) in Brussels, IRNA reported on Friday.
 The MOU, which is considered a turning point in two countries’ customs cooperation, was signed on the sidelines of a WCO special session and in the presence of head of Islamic Republic of Iran Customs Administration (IRICA) and his Tajik counterpart.  Iran and Tajikistan had signed a customs cooperation agreement back in 2002, the same report confirmed.
