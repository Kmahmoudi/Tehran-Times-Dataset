TEHRAN- The Iranian cabinet in their Sunday evening meeting appointed Saeed Mohammadzadeh as the new head as well as board of directors’ chairman of Islamic Republic of Iran Railways (IRIR), replacing Mohsen Pour Seyed Aqaei, IRNA reported.  During the meeting, Iranian President Hassan Rouhani underscored the vitality of developing Iran’s railway network through attracting foreign investments.
Mohammadzadeh has previously been the deputy head of IRIR for technical and infrastructural affairs.  HJ/MA All Content by Mehr News Agency is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.
