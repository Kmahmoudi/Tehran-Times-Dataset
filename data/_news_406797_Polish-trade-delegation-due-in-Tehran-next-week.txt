TEHRAN- A 50-member Polish trade delegation headed by the country’s deputy minister for economic development will leave Poland for Tehran on October 2, Iran Chamber of Commerce, Industries, Mines and Agriculture (ICCIMA) announced on Sunday.  The delegation, comprised of Polish officials and managers of economic enterprises, aims for development of economic relationship with Iran. Representatives of the Polish companies accompanying the country’s deputy minister plan to hold talks with Iranian counterparts to investigate possible opportunities for joint investments. The companies present in the delegation are active in the fields of ICT, LPG and CNG systems, accessories, oil and gas pipelines, electrical connections, insurance, sport goods, food, mining, geology and drilling, food processing, industrial machinery, pharmaceutical and medical products and etc. A joint business forum is also scheduled for the mentioned date at the building of ICCIMA in Tehran.
