It was a dinner hosted in honor of Pakistan Foreign Secretary Salman Bashir at the High Commissioner's residence at Delhi. The talks between India's Foreign Secretary Nirupama Rao and him had ended without any concrete result. I asked Bashir at the dinner whether Kashmir figured in their discussions. He said, “Yes”. But the most significant remark he made was in reply to my query if the ground covered behind-the-scenes would have to be walked over once again. He said they would pick up the thread from where it had been let off. “We would resume from the stage already reached,” Bashir said.
