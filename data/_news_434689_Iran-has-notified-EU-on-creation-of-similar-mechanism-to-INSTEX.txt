 INSTEX is a special purpose vehicle aimed at facilitating legitimate trade between European economic operators and Iran after the U.S. withdrawal from the 2015 nuclear deal and reimposition of sanctions. France, Germany and Britain announced the creation of INSTEX on January 31. European Union foreign policy chief Federica Mogherini, French Foreign Minister Jean-Yves Le Drian, German Foreign Minister Heiko Maas and British Foreign Secretary Jeremy Hunt have said INSTEX will support legitimate European trade with Iran.
