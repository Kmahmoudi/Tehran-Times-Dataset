TEHRAN – The head of the Atomic Energy Organization of Iran has announced that the country will receive 130 tons of uranium in the near future to increase its reserves to 350 tons.

 “130 tons of uranium will soon arrive in the country according to an agreement made by members of the JCPOA Joint Commission,” Ali Akbar Salehi said on Sunday, addressing a ceremony in the central city of Natanz which is home to uranium enrichment facilities. SP/PA
