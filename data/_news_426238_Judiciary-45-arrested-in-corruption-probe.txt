TEHRAN – Judiciary spokesman Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i said on Monday that so far 45 people have been arrested as part of a probe into the high volatility in the foreign currency market. “So far, 45 individuals have been arrested over their role in the foreign currency market,” Mohseni-Eje'i said, ISNA reported. He added that a number of them are still under custody, and some of them have been freed on bail. “Yesterday (Sunday), seven people including a former deputy central bank governor and five foreign exchange dealers were arrested for alleged economic crimes,” the spokesman explained.
