TEHRAN- According to OPEC’s latest monthly report published on April 13, Iran’s oil production, based on secondary sources, stood at 3.814 million barrels per day (bpd) in March, up 3,300 bpd from that of February. The country produced about 3.810 million barrels per day of crude oil in February, the report read. The OPEC’s report didn’t provide any record for Iran’s oil output based on direct communication in the mentioned month. The report also presented the country’s average heavy oil price to be $61.15 in March, a $1.12 or 1.7 percent fall from $62.27 in February. According to the report, Iran’s year to date average heavy crude price stood at $63.48 almost $11.8 more than the figure for last year’s same time span.
