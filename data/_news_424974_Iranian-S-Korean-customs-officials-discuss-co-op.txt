TEHRAN- Heads of Islamic Republic of Iran Customs Administration (IRICA) and Korea Customs Service conferred on mutual business collaboration and removal of trade barriers on the sidelines of Annual World Customs Organization (WCO) meeting in the Belgian capital on Saturday, IRIB news reported. The two officials also discussed customs data exchange under mutual administrative agreements, the report added. Before this meeting, IRICA Head Foruod Asgari had held separate meetings with the Chinese minister of General Administration of Customs and his Japanese, Malaysian and Australian counterparts on the sidelines of the same event. Iran could be accepted as a member of WCO Policy Commission regarding the vote of the international body members during the WCO meeting.
