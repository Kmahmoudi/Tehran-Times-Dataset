TEHRAN – Iran’s third specialized trade exhibition in Kabul, opened yesterday, would wrap up on March 5, the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting (IRIB) reported on Wednesday. During the exhibition, Iranian firms would showcase their products in the fields of mining, energy, automobile, civil services, telecommunication, housing and construction. Food stuff, home appliances, rugs and carpets, medicine and medical equipment are among the products on display. Meanwhile, Afghan Commercial Attaché in Iran Abdolhamid Farhang said Afghans have a preference for Iranian goods, yet, the companies need to bring their most qualified products into display in order to play in the market. Afghanistan was the fourth largest importer of Iranian goods in the first 11 months of the current Iranian calendar year (March 21, 2015-February 19, 2016), Iran’s Customs Administration reported. Iran has set a target of $77.5 billion in non-oil exports for the current Iranian calendar year, according to Mojtaba Khosrotaj, the Iranian deputy industry, mining, and trade minister.            SJ/MA/ All Content by Mehr News Agency is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.
