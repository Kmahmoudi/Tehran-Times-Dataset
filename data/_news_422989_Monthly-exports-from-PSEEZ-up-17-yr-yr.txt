TEHRAN- The value of exports from Iran’s Pars Special Economic Energy Zone (PSEEZ) rose by 17 percent in the first month of the current Iranian calendar year (March 21-April 20), compared to the same month in the previous year. Exports from the zone, which is located in southwest of the country, stood at over 2.359 million tons worth more than $1.249 billion during the mentioned month, IRNA reported on Wednesday quoting Ahmad Pour-Heidar, the director general of the zone’s customs department.
