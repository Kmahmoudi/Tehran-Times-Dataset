TEHRAN – Iranian Foreign Minister Mohammad Javad Zarif is scheduled to first visit Beijing and Moscow and then fly to Brussels for consultations on the nuclear deal, known as the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA). “Zarif will start his diplomatic tour on Sunday to discuss the JCPOA. The trip will begin with China, which he will visit on Sunday and Monday, then Zarif will travel to Russia and meet with his counterpart. Then he will go to Brussels to meet with his European counterparts,” Sputnik quoted an Iranian Foreign Ministry source as saying on Friday.
