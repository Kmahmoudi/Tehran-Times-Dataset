TEHRAN - Hamid Pour-Mohammadi has been appointed as the new governor of the Central Bank of Iran (CBI), replacing Abdolnasser Hemmati, who is running for the upcoming presidential elections, Mehr reported on Sunday citing an informed source. However, the central bank has not yet confirmed the appointment. Pour-Mohammadi was previously the deputy director of the Planning and Budget Organization (PBO) for economic affairs. Reportedly, President Hassan Rouhani has asked Hemmati to resign as the CBI governor to focus on his election campaigns.  EF/MG All Content by Mehr News Agency is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.
