TEHRAN – Vice President Es’haq Jahangiri says the Foreign Ministry has been tasked with opening a European Union (EU) office in Tehran, Tasnim news agency reported. In a meeting with the European Union Commissioner for Agriculture and Rural Development Phil Hogan on Saturday, Jahangiri described Iran as a reliable partner for the EU. He further urged the EU to expand cooperation with Tehran, saying, “Iran has a long history of economic cooperation with European Union countries, and the majority of reputable European firms have had a presence in Iran.” MH/SP All Content by Mehr News Agency is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.
