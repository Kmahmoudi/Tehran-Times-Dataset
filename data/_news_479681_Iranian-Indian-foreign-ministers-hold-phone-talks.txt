TEHRAN - To advance relations between Tehran and New Delhi it is necessary to implement the agreements between Iran and India, Iranian Foreign Minister Hossein Amir Abdollahian emphasized on Monday. Amir Abdollahian made the remarks in a phone conversation with his Indian counterpart Subrahmanyam Jaishankar. Amir Abdollahian and Jaishankar discussed topics related to bilateral ties as well as regional and global concerns of relevance. The necessity to increase collaboration in several areas was stressed by the foreign ministers of the two nations. Jaishankar discussed Tehran-New Delhi's bilateral and global collaboration while referring to the positive state of the two nations' ties and cooperation.
