Here we go again. The Special Tribunal for Lebanon, a United Nations-backed body investigating the killing of Lebanese Prime Minister Rafiq Hariri in 2005, has accused four people of his murder. They all belong to Hezbollah, the Lebanese Shia movement that Israel and the United States define as terrorist. But they are probably not guilty.
