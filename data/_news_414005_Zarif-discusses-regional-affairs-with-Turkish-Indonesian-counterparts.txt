TEHRAN – Iran’s Foreign Minister Mohammad Javad Zarif has talked over the telephone with his Turkish and Indonesian counterparts, Mehr news agency reported on Monday.

 His first call was made with Turkish Foreign Minister Mevlut Cavusoglu to discuss Tehran and Ankara’s views on the latest developments of concern to the neighboring countries. In another call to Retno Marsudi, Zarif discussed similar issues, which included changes in political relations between some Arab states including between Qatar and Saudi Arabia. SP/MH
