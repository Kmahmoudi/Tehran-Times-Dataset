"I see it as unlikely that negotiations with the U.S.... will amount to something considering America's destructive measures and its political confusion in Iraq," said Ali Akbar Velayati, a top advisor on foreign affairs to Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. "The Americans have proved that they are not honest. It is simplistic to hope that Iran-U.S. talks would resolve the Iraqi people's problems," said Velayati, who was also the Islamic Republic's longest-serving foreign minister.
