TEHRAN – Responding to recent allegations by the British government against the Islamic Republic, Tehran Prosecutor Abbas Jafari Dolatabadi has said Nazanin Zaghari-Ratcliffe and other convicts being held in Iranian prisons for security offenses are provided with the best conditions, including using phone calls, meeting with families and medical care. Speaking with a number of reporters in a local ceremony on Wednesday, Jafari Dolatabadi called on Western governments to stop interfering in the judicial affairs of Iran, saying, “Zagari-Ratcliffe and several other security convicts are kept in prison for very serious offences,” Mehr reported.
